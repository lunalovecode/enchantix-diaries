September 3

Dear Diary,

It's my first day at Enchantix Academy. And I'm on my own...

I went through all of the classes. No one noticed me. That's fine. I'd rather be invisible than a laughingstock.

Or, at least, I thought I was invisible.

"Hi!" a girl with pink curly hair greeted. I nearly dropped you, Diary, but I managed to catch you in time. "I'm Blossom."

"Oh... Hi..." I greeted back, trying to process what just happened.

"Are you okay?" she asked.

"I'm just surprised," I replied. "No one bothers to talk to me. And when I make an effort, the person I'm trying to befriend ignores me."

I immediately realized that I should not have said that.

"You're Seo Hyun-mi, right?"

"Mhm."

"What kind of powers do you have?" she asked.

Shoot. Should I just lie? No. It'll be even worse when she finds out I lied. I guess I'll tell the truth, then.

"Are you sure you want to know? This is the part where everyone goes away."

"Why would I go away?"

"Because of, well, this..."

I formed a ball of shadow in my hands. Instead of running away, she seemed to be fascinated by it.

"You're not scared?" I asked. "That's dark magic. I—"

"Having dark magic doesn't make you a bad person."

"I know, but people don't think that's true. Just look."

A group of three older girls, one with silver-blue hair tied in a high ponytail, another with long brown hair with two lighter brown streaks tied into braids, and yet another with a high dark purple hairstyle with light purple steaks on the sides, teased another girl with short fiery orange hair. The victim drove them away with an explosion of multicolored flames.

"You're not like them," she reassured me.

"Thanks... But I better get going."

I picked my pen up and went back to the dorms.

"Good luck!"

Why is Blossom being so nice to me? What could she possibly want with powers like mine?

Never mind. It doesn't matter now. Time to do my homework, I guess.

Love, Hyun-mi

Monday, October 28

Dear Diary,

I woke up in the dungeon of the Dark Fairy dorms.

"Looks like all of the Light Fairies are locked up," I heard Khione say.

"I'm a Dark Fairy. Why are you locking me up?" I demanded.

"You're so soft, you might as well be a Light Fairy."

"Blossom!" I called when I saw her walk in.

Blossom gave me a glare with glowing purple eyes. No. They couldn't have done this. They couldn't have.

"I hope you rot in this cell."

She wouldn't say that. She would never say that.

"Dragon Essence."

Helia trapped Soleil in a tornado of fire.

When the tornado died down, Ariadne said, "Cursed Lightning!"

I saw Soleil's eyes turn red for a second.

"Succumb, Soleil," Ariadne taunted.

"I—No, it's too crazy. I don't know where to go from here..."

"What's your idea?"

I whispered, "I thought maybe I could disguise you a Dark Fairy. Maybe they'd let you out and... I don't know. It's probably not the best idea."

"It's an idea. It's worth a shot!"

She had a point. "Okay. Hold on."

What would a Light Fairy look like as a Dark Fairy? She has her Enchantix... Maybe a darker version of her dress? Red eyes, maybe? Jagged wings? Then again, if I haven't seen her transform into a Dark Fairy, no one else has.

"Ready?" I asked.

"Wait, what if they try to read my mind?"

"Then I'll use a mind-reading spell of my own. None of us are skilled enough to read someone's mind when it's already being read."

"Huh."

"Heads up, it's going to feel a bit weird. 3, 2, 1, Link Minds."

Sparks of electricity hit my temples. From the way Soleil jolted upright, it looked like it worked.

"...That is not how I expected it to feel like," Soleil thought. "But I think we're settled!"

I closed my eyes and coated her with a wave of energy. When I opened them, Soleil's appearance changed to the one I pictured.

"Ha! I told you," Ariadne said to Persephone.

Persephone said nothing and unlocked Soleil's cell. Soleil walked out, pushing Persephone aside and looking down on the other students.

"I am not an actress..." she thought. "Oh, Blossom's here. I'll try to break her out."

"Ugh, they really need to renew this spell..." she said aloud.

For a split second, light shot of her hands, which I quickly changed to look like darkness.

"Pretend that nothing happened," she thought. I assumed that's what she was whispering to Blossom.

"HELIA! Did you undo the hypnosis?!"

"Fine! I DID snap them out of it!"

Helia was taking the blame?

"Go ahead! Throw me in a cell! See if I care!"

They grabbed Helia and locked her in the cell Soleil had been in.

"Look, I'm s—" I started to apologize.

"Willpower."

Helia shot me with a burst of blue light. It gave me more strength to keep the illusion up.

"Thanks..."

"Honestly, that's impressive. How'd you keep both of those spells for that long?"

"Concentration. A lot of concentration."
